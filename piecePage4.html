OVERVIEW

Concept

A minute and a half landing page video for The Revalie Ottawa, geared towards university students. The video explores the benefits of living at The Revalie through examples of the amenities offered and the sense of community.


The video was done as a final group project for an Audio and Video class, where we had to film and edit an advertisement or short story.

REFLECTION

Takeaways

Learned to use audio recording tools. I was responsible for recording the interview audio. To record the audio, we used a Zoom H4N Portable Recorder and a Lavalier Mic - Sennheiser EV122. I became familiar with these tools and how they work together.

Constantly monitored the exposure triangle. When filming, I kept an eye on the aperture and ISO and adjusted them as the lighting changed.

Became more familiar with Premiere Pro. I became familiar with many tools offered in Premiere Pro including Essential Sound, Essential Graphics, Effects and sound editing. When editing I also used Lumetri Scopes to monitor and adjust the exposure and saturation of each shot to ensure consistency.

Became familiar with Adobe Audition. There was some mic scratching and background noise in the raw sound recording, so I used Adobe Audition to minimise it.

Focused on editing and flow. I kept the duration of each shot to around 2 seconds to create and preserve the upbeat mood of the video.
